## What worked

- `gen_all_scales.sh` ran cleanly, didn't trip its disk-cap check.
- Both phases (tpchgen-cli parquet → polars/numpy encode) completed without
  OOM despite the encode RSS peaking at ~565 GB. We have 1 TB host RAM so
  this is comfortable; on a 256 GB box this would be a problem at SF300.

## What failed

Nothing.

## Surprises

- **The encode rate is the bottleneck.** Both SF50 (0.17 GB/s) and SF300
  (0.16 GB/s) encode at the same rate, *much slower* than NVMe write or
  parquet generation. At SF1000 this projects to ~75 min for encode alone.
  This is purely a code bottleneck — the encode is a Python loop calling
  numpy, hitting GIL and cache-miss patterns. The `tpchgen-cli` parquet
  generation itself is *fast* (took ~8 min for 27 GB at SF100, ~10 min for
  82 GB at SF300 — i.e. ~150 MB/s, also the disk-write ceiling).

- **RSS went to 565 GB.** Not a problem with 1 TB RAM, but means the SF1000
  encode (3.3× more rows) would peak at ~1.9 TB — *would not fit* even on
  this box. Need to either chunk the polars dataframe or stream encoding.
  Filed as 1.1.1 below.

## Interpretation

SF300 generation is a one-time 24 min cost we'll pay multiple times this
week (re-baselining, ablation runs, OS reboots). It is **not** scaled-up
from production data, but synthetic TPC-H — for the paper that's fine, but
real-world experiments (Tier 5) will need different gen pipelines. The
encode-rate ceiling at 0.16 GB/s is a forward-looking issue: SF1000+ needs
a chunked encoder before we even attempt to generate it.

## Follow-ups

- **1.1.1 (new) — chunked / streaming encode in `gen_tpch_fast.py`.** Read
  parquet in row-group-sized chunks, encode each into a slab of the mmap,
  release. Should drop peak RSS from N×120B to one row-group's worth and
  unlock SF1000+ on 256 GB / 512 GB host machines (and avoid the 1 TB cliff
  on this box).
- **1.1.2 (new) — bypass parquet entirely.** Pipe tpchgen-cli's TBL output
  straight into our encoder. Saves the full 25-90 GB parquet round-trip on
  /mnt/data (which we then immediately delete).
