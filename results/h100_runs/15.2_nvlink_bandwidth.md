## What worked

- **NVLink is uniform across all pairs at 133.3 GB/s/direction.** No
  asymmetry between same-NUMA pairs (GPU0+1, GPU2+3) and cross-NUMA
  pairs (e.g., GPU0+2). This is the signature of a true all-to-all
  NVLink topology — confirmed by 15.1's `nvidia-smi topo -m` (NV6
  between every pair).
- 133.3 GB/s achieved out of 150 GB/s theoretical (NV6 = 6 links ×
  25 GB/s/link/direction) = **89% of NVLink ceiling**. Healthy for
  cudaMemcpyPeer (some launch + sync overhead).
- Intra-GPU memcpy hits 994 GB/s — close to the H100 NVL HBM3 spec of
  2 TB/s when accounting for the read+write of each byte during
  memcpy (effective 1 TB/s per direction, ✓).

## What failed

Nothing.

## Surprises

1. **NVLink p2p is uniform across all pairs.** I expected the cross-
   NUMA pairs (GPU0↔GPU2/3, GPU1↔GPU2/3) to be slower because they
   cross the inter-socket bus. Actually no — NVLink is a direct GPU-
   to-GPU link that bypasses the CPU/NUMA fabric entirely. The CPU
   topology only matters for HOST-side traffic.

2. **Aggregate bandwidth is huge.** 4 GPUs × 133 GB/s outbound each =
   **533 GB/s aggregate** if all 4 push concurrently. For comparison,
   the PCIe 5 x16 link to host is ~32 GB/s — so all-to-all NVLink
   transfer is **17× faster than PCIe to host.** This drives the
   multi-GPU sort design: stage cross-GPU shuffles via NVLink, never
   touch the host for inter-GPU communication.

3. **Single-pair NVLink (133 GB/s) is *much faster* than HBM-to-pinned-
   host PCIe (~25 GB/s effective).** Implication: for any
   GPU-to-GPU exchange, NVLink is 5× faster than a host roundtrip.

## Interpretation

The hardware is in excellent shape for multi-GPU sort. The NVLink mesh
gives ~530 GB/s aggregate inter-GPU bandwidth, which is ~17× the PCIe
ceiling and ~30× the slow-path key upload PCIe rate currently
bottlenecking SF50/100 (which see ~16-21 GB/s effective).

Concretely for the 4-GPU SF500 plan (15.4):
- Each GPU starts with 1/4 of input (~90 GB)
- After local sort, NVLink-shuffle records to their target bucket
- Per-GPU outbound: ~67 GB (3/4 of records leave) at 133 GB/s = 0.5 s
- Compared to alternative: each GPU writes its output to host = 90 GB /
  25 GB/s = 3.6 s
- **NVLink-based shuffle is 7× faster than host-mediated shuffle.**
