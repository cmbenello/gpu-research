## Why SF300 has the best throughput

Counterintuitively, SF300 (27.8 GB/s) is faster than SF50 (25.5 GB/s).
Reason: at SF300 the **OVC architecture** kicks in (chunked LSD + GPU
prefix merge + CPU gather), which does more bytes per phase but
avoids the all-host-RAM gather that dominates SF50/SF100. The gather
phase still runs (line "Gathered 216.00 GB in 3972 ms (54.37 GB/s)"
in the SF300 trace), but is a smaller fraction of total wall.

Looking at SF300 numactl trace breakdown:
- Phase 1 (run gen): 3177 ms (67.99 GB/s)
- Phase 2 (GPU OVC merge): 580 ms (1300 GB/s on HBM keys)
- Phase 3 (CPU gather): 3972 ms (54.37 GB/s)
- Phase 4 (CPU fixup): 2 ms

So gather is 51% of warm wall. Numactl improves gather throughput
from ~30 GB/s (default) to ~54 GB/s. That's the source of the 1.73×
warm speedup at SF300.

## Caveats

- All numbers are single-sweep. Need ≥3 sweeps to bound CIs.
- The default SF300 sweep had **5042 → 18192 ms** range across 4
  warm runs. That's so noisy the "median 13490 ms" is barely
  representative. Better to characterize default as
  "5042-18192, no stable mode."
- The numactl SF300 cold run (19730 ms) is 6.4× slower than its
  warm runs. Cold includes file I/O from NVMe (216 GB at ~3 GB/s
  = 72 sec just for read, plus first-touch faulting).
- These numbers exclude verify (--no-verify). Adding verification
  costs another ~2-3 sec.

## Update FINAL throughput table

The previous `THROUGHPUT_TABLE.md` listed:
- SF50: 1.51 s

These were obtained without numactl; the new numbers are:
- **SF50: 1.41 s** (1.07× faster)
- **SF100: 2.95 s** (1.02× faster)
- **SF300: 7.78 s** (1.11× faster)

The improvements are smaller than the median speedups because the
old "warm best" numbers were already the best of 5 (which happened
to land near-optimal). With numactl the 4 warm runs are tightly
clustered, so even the median is ~best-of-5.
