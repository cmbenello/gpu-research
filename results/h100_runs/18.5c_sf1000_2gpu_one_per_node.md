## Why 2-GPU one-per-node beats 4-GPU paired

Per-bucket throughput:

| Method | Per-bucket wall | per-GPU GB/s |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| 17.3.2.7.6 paired (2 GPUs/node) | 42-45 sec | 4.0-4.3 |
| **18.5c one-per-node (this)** | **24-26 sec round 1, 55-59 round 2** | **7.0-7.4 round 1** |

Round 1 of one-per-node is **1.7× faster than 4-GPU paired**, matching
the well-documented pattern: 2-per-node loses 35% to memory contention.

But round 2 has the cache-pollution slowdown. Average across both
rounds: 41 sec/bucket = 4.4 GB/s — essentially the same as 4-GPU
paired. So the *aggregate* doesn't beat 4-GPU paired by much (both
~12 min sort phase).

The win comes from the simpler model: **one-per-node + pre-partition
= 6m14s sort phase on SF1000.**

## Path to a faster SF1000

The 2.3× round-2 slowdown is from page cache. Eliminating it would
give ~24 sec × 4 buckets / 2 (still concurrent) = ~50 sec sort phase.
**Theoretical pre-partitioned SF1000: 50 sec (~1 min)** if the
cache could be pre-emptied between rounds.

Or, use `posix_fadvise(POSIX_FADV_DONTNEED)` after each bucket finishes
to evict its pages. Quick experiment.
