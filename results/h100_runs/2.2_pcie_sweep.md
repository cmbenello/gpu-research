## What worked

- The PCIe compression ratio **jumps at SF300** (1.71× → 3.33×) when the
  OVC architecture engages. That's the first scale where the compact
  upload path (32 B keys) replaces the full-key upload (66 B per record).
- USE_BITPACK adds another ~1× compression on top of OVC compact (3.33×
  → 4.29× at SF300) by uploading 24 B keys instead of 32 B.
- The bitpack-vs-baseline gap *only opens at SF300+*, exactly matching
  the story from 0.2/0.3: at smaller scales the slow-path upload doesn't
  honor the smaller key, so bitpack has no effect.

## What failed

SF500 and SF1000 both FAIL before producing any PCIe number. The
chart annotates these as missing data points.

## Surprises

1. **There's a sharp transition at SF50/100 → SF300, not a gradual curve.**
   Below the OVC threshold, PCIe is dominated by the strided full-key
   upload (always 66 B × records, regardless of bitpack). Above the
   threshold, compact upload kicks in and saves 50-75% of bytes-on-the-
   wire. **Filed as 0.3.1** — fixing the sub-OVC path to also use compact
   upload would smooth this curve and give SF50/100 a 2× PCIe-traffic
   improvement.

2. **The download is asymmetric.** D→H is just the permutation (4 B per
   record at SF50+), not the sorted records — that's because the sort
   leaves records in place on host and only downloads a permutation. So
   D→H is 1.2 GB (SF50) regardless of compression strategy.

3. **At SF300 the entire 216 GB sort moves through a 50 GB PCIe channel** —
   ratio 4.29×. At PCIe 5 ceiling 32 GB/s, that's a 1.6 s minimum PCIe
   time. We measured 8.94 s warm-best at SF300 → PCIe is ~18% of total
   wall time, the rest is GPU sort + CPU gather.

## Interpretation

PCIe compression is the **headline architectural advantage** of this sort
relative to a naive GPU radix sort that uploads full records. At SF300+
we're moving *less than a quarter of the raw bytes* through the PCIe5
link. That means: as long as the OVC architecture is in play, scaling
the data by 10× costs only ~3× more PCIe — sub-linear in the only
expensive transport channel.

The clear gap at SF50/SF100 (where bitpack does nothing) is **not a
hardware limitation** but a code-path bug — it would close after the
0.3.1 fix.
