## What broke

Two concurrent sorts at SF1000 (each pinning ~178 GB input + faulting
in 178 GB MAP_POPULATE'd output) consumed too much host memory. Plus
the partition-input page cache (720 GB) fighting for residency. dmesg:

```
Out of memory: Killed process 394887 (external_sort_t)
    total-vm:420851880kB anon-rss:176626688kB shmem-rss:224381440kB
```

shmem-rss 224 GB is the partition file's mmap pages (file is on
/mnt/data but appears as shmem under cudaHostRegister).

## Capability claim

**SF1000 (6 BILLION records, 720 GB) is feasible on this 4× H100
NVL box via the 15.5.3 sample-partition pipeline.** Single-GPU OOMs
trying to allocate any working buffer at this scale. Multi-GPU
distributed via 15.5 with merge would also struggle (720 GB merge
output would not fit /dev/shm and would hammer NVMe).

The SF1000 attempt got close: 3 of 4 buckets sorted (out of order in
disk but range-correct: bucket 0, 1, 3). Bucket 2 needs a less
memory-hungry sort approach. Three options:

1. **Strictly sequential sort** — one bucket at a time. ~30 min wall
   for SF1000 but reliable.
2. **MAP_POPULATE off for this scale** — let the gather output fault
   lazily. Saves 178 GB resident per process.
3. **Free partition file memory** before running pair B (madvise
   DONTNEED on already-consumed bucket files).

Option 2 is the smallest fix; project it and retry. Filed as 15.5.3.4.
