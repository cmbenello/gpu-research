## Why this beats 19.15 K=8 2-GPU

| Approach              | # buckets | Bucket size | Concurrency | Rounds | Sort phase |
|-----------------------|-----------|-------------|-------------|--------|------------|
| 19.15 K=8 + 2-GPU     | 8         | 135 GB      | 2 buckets/round | 4 | 18m08s |
| 19.15.1 K=16 + 2-GPU  | 16        | 67 GB       | 2 buckets/round | 8 | 17m12s |
| **19.16 K=16 + 4-GPU**| **16**    | **67 GB**   | **4 buckets/round** | **4** | **12m04s** |

The win is that **4-GPU concurrency saturates the host PCIe + NVMe bandwidth**
across all 4 GPUs, while the per-bucket overhead (file I/O, host pin, kernel
launch) stays roughly constant per round. With 4× the work in only 1.4× the
round time, total sort phase drops by 5+ minutes.

## Why 19.12 (K=8 + 4-GPU) failed but 19.16 (K=16 + 4-GPU) succeeds

19.12 failed with cudaMallocHost "invalid argument" because:
- 4 buckets × 135 GB = 540 GB pinned simultaneously
- Plus 575 GB OS page cache from partition output
- Combined ~1.1 TB > 1 TB host RAM → cudaMallocHost couldn't allocate

19.16 succeeds because:
- posix_fadvise(DONTNEED) drops cache to <1 GB before sort phase
- 4 buckets × 67 GB = 268 GB pinned simultaneously
- Plus minimal cache pollution
- Combined ~270 GB << 1 TB → easy fit

The K=16 + cache eviction combo is **necessary** for 4-GPU at SF1500.
