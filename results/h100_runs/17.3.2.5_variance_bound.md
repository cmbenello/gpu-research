# 17.3.2.5 — Variance bound: 2-sweep confirmation of numactl 1.7× speedup

**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Workload:** SF100, two independent --runs 5 sweeps each (default + numactl)
**Status:** PASS — speedup is robust at ~1.7× (slight downward revision from 1.84×)

## Combined data (2 sweeps × 4 warm runs = 8 warm runs each)

### Default warm walls
```
Sweep 1: 5042, 5480, 5639, 6157   (range 1115 ms)
Sweep 2: 3342, 4944, 4998, 5392   (range 2050 ms)
Combined median: 5070 ms
Combined range: 3342 - 6157 = 2815 ms
```

### Numactl warm walls
```
Sweep 1: 2952, 2963, 2980, 2987   (range 35 ms)
Sweep 2: 2941, 2948, 2974, 2980   (range 39 ms)
Combined median: 2967 ms
Combined range: 2941 - 2987 = 46 ms
```

## Result

| Statistic | Default | Numactl | Speedup |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Best of 8 warm | 3342 ms | 2941 ms | 1.14× |
| Median of 8 warm | **5070 ms** | **2967 ms** | **1.71×** |
| Worst of 8 warm | 6157 ms | 2987 ms | 2.06× |
| Range | 2815 ms | 46 ms | 61× tighter |

## Interpretation

- **Median 1.71× speedup** is the most defensible claim. Tighter
  than the single-sweep 1.84× number from 17.3.2.1.
- **Default variance is huge** (range 2815 ms = ~50% of median).
  The kernel's auto-NUMA migration is non-deterministic.
- **Numactl variance is essentially zero** (46 ms range = 1.5% of
  median). Bind everything → reproducible.

## Why the 1.84× from 17.3.2.1 was a slight overshoot

The original claim used sweep 1's median 5480 ms vs numactl 2980 ms =
1.84×. With 2 sweeps combined, default median drops to 5070 ms (one
of sweep 2's warm runs hit 3342 ms, pulling the median down). The
true median speedup is closer to 1.71×.

For paper claims, **report 1.7× SF100 wall reduction with numactl
(median across 8 warm runs)**. The tightness of numactl's variance
is the more compelling story than the exact multiplier.

## Variance comparison story (the real headline)

| Config | StdDev / Median | Coefficient of variation |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Default warm | ~700 ms / 5070 = 13.8% | very high |
| Numactl warm | ~16 ms / 2967 = 0.5% | nearly deterministic |

**Numactl makes the sort 27× more reproducible.** Even if the
absolute median speedup is only 1.7×, the noise reduction alone
is paper-worthy: "Our sort runs in 2.97 ± 0.02 sec under numactl
vs 5.07 ± 0.7 sec under default" is a much stronger claim than
just the speedup.

## Updated headline number

| Scale | Old (single sweep) | New (2 sweeps median) |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|
| SF100 numactl warm | 2980 ms (1.84×) | **2967 ms (1.71×)** |

## Build & run

```bash
# Two independent sweeps:
./external_sort_tpch_compact --input lineitem_sf100.bin --runs 5
numactl --cpunodebind=0 --membind=0 ./external_sort_tpch_compact \
    --input lineitem_sf100.bin --runs 5
```

## Caveats

- Only 2 sweeps. Need ≥5 sweeps for true CI bounding.
- Both sweeps were within 30 minutes of each other; system page-
  cache state was likely correlated.
- Default variance hints at kernel NUMA migration — disabling
  `kernel.numa_balancing` (sudo) might tighten default variance.
