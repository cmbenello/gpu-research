## What worked

- Generation completed without disk-cap or OOM trips.
- The encode rate held steady at ~0.15-0.16 GB/s across SF50/SF100/SF300/SF500 —
  good news for SF1000 projection (~75 min for encode if scaling holds).

## What failed

Nothing.

## Surprises

- **Encode rate is *almost* constant across scales (0.15-0.17 GB/s).** That
  means the encode step is throughput-bound, not latency-bound — so there's
  no per-row overhead amplifying at scale. Implication: the optimization
  in 1.1.1 (chunked / streaming encode) gives the same relative wins at
  every scale.

- **Peak RSS climbed from 565 GB (SF300) to 686 GB (SF500).** Linear with
  data size — which means SF1000 will peak at ~1.2 TB host RAM. We have
  1024 GB total. **SF1000 won't fit through this encoder unless we chunk
  it.** Filed under 1.1.1 already; this is now a hard blocker for 1.5/1.6.

## Interpretation

SF500 generation works fine on this 1 TB / 3.5 TB box but at 43 min per gen
it's the slowest fixed cost we'll pay this week. Combined with peak-RSS
scaling, **SF1000 cannot be generated through the current
gen_tpch_fast.py**: peak RSS would exceed available memory. The 1.5
generate_sf1000 queue item should be marked `[!]` until 1.1.1 (chunked
encode) lands.
