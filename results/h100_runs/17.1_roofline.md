## Sort-specific operations and their bottlenecks

### Operation: per-record CPU compact extract (0.3.1 path)
- 26 indirect 1-byte reads + 32 byte writes per record
- Memory traffic: ~98 B per record
- 600 M records (SF100): 58 GB of traffic
- Host RAM ceiling: ~400 GB/s DDR5
- Theoretical: 58 / 400 = 0.15 s
- **Measured: 0.5-0.7 s** = ~25% of host memory bandwidth efficiency
- Headroom: 4× possible with better cache/SIMD utilization

### Operation: PCIe upload of compact keys (post-0.3.1)
- 32 B per record uploaded H2D
- 600 M records (SF100): 19.2 GB
- PCIe 5 ceiling: 32 GB/s
- Theoretical: 0.6 s
- **Measured: ~0.8 s** = 75% of PCIe ceiling
- Headroom: 1.3× possible

### Operation: GPU LSD radix sort (4 passes on 32 B compact key)
- Per pass: read N×8 B keys + read N×4 B perm + write N×8 B keys + write N×4 B perm = 24 B/record
- 4 passes: 96 B/record traffic
- 600 M records: 57.6 GB HBM traffic per sort
- HBM3 ceiling: 3.35 TB/s
- Theoretical: 0.017 s = 17 ms
- **Measured: ~80 ms** for 4 passes on 600 M
- We're at ~21% of HBM peak — typical for CUB radix sort. Not the bottleneck.

### Operation: CPU gather (random read, sequential NT write)
- Random reads: 600 M × 120 B = 72 GB at random addresses
- Sequential NT writes: 600 M × 120 B = 72 GB
- Total memory traffic: 144 GB at SF100, 432 GB at SF300
- Host RAM ceiling: ~400 GB/s
- Theoretical: 0.36 s at SF100, 1.08 s at SF300
- **Measured at SF300: 4.8 s** = ~22% of theoretical ceiling
- Random reads are the killer (cache miss penalty)
- **Headroom: 4-5× possible** with two-pass gather (sort perm by source for sequential reads)

### Operation: 4-way k-way merge (CPU multi-threaded)
- Read K streams + write 1 output: 2× total bytes
- 360 GB at SF500: 720 GB total memory traffic
- Host RAM ceiling: ~400 GB/s
- Theoretical: 720 / 400 = 1.8 s
- **Measured at SF300 in /dev/shm: 5.61 GB/s on 216 GB = 38.5 s**
- For SF500 with NVMe-backed output, NVMe write at 3 GB/s caps at 2 min
- 64-thread merge = ~14% of memory bandwidth ceiling
- Headroom: 3-7× possible with NUMA-aware placement

## Where we are vs the roofline

The H100 sort is at **~30% of the theoretical machine peak** at SF300.
Single biggest gap: **Phase 3 CPU gather at 11%**. Closing this to
~50% (with two-pass gather + NUMA-partition) would drop SF300 wall
from 8.65 s to ~5 s — and free up the next bottleneck (Phase 1 PCIe).

For multi-GPU at SF500:
- Aggregate sort throughput measured: 53.2 GB/s
- Aggregate HBM ceiling: 4 × 1 TB/s = 4 TB/s
- Aggregate PCIe ceiling: 4 × 25 GB/s = 100 GB/s
- We're at ~50% of PCIe ceiling, 1.3% of aggregate HBM
- **PCIe is the binding constraint for distributed sort**, not HBM

## Predicted ceilings if we fix everything

## Conclusion

The single-GPU sort is currently **PCIe-bound for the upload phase
(~60% efficient) and CPU-memory-bound for the gather phase (~11%
efficient)**. The roofline says there's roughly 2× headroom on
single-GPU and 7× headroom on multi-GPU — the latter dominated by
fixing the disk-staging in the distributed sort merge.

**Highest-ROI next optimization is two-pass gather** (Phase 3 efficiency
11% → 50%) — single-GPU SF300 wall drops from 8.65 s → ~5 s.
