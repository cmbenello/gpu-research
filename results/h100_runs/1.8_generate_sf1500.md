## What worked

- The chunked encoder (1.1.1) made SF1500 possible. Peak RSS 696 GB
  vs projected ~1.5+ TB without chunking — would have OOM'd python.
- Encode rate held at 0.21 GB/s same as SF1000, confirming the per-row-group
  encoder is throughput-stable across scales.
- The disk-cap check (80%) was approached during the parquet phase
  (peak 79%) but never tripped. The ~440 GB parquet was cleaned up
  immediately after the encode completed.

## What failed

Nothing.

## Surprises

1. **RSS still drifted to 696 GB at SF1500** despite the chunked encoder.
   Higher than SF1000's 594 GB peak (~1.18× scale ratio matches the
   1.5× data scale ratio). The 1.5.1 follow-up (gc.collect between
   row groups, committed at 23:34 — *after* this gen started) should
   address it on the next run.

2. **`tpchgen-cli` SF1500 row count is 9.000 B, not the theoretical 9.002
   B** (sf × 6,001,215). About 1.8 M rows fewer than the formula. This is
   tpchgen-cli's quirk (stable seed + per-SF random sampling). The
   gen_all_scales.sh table should be updated to match. Filed as 1.8.1
   below.

3. **The total 1.5 TB of TPC-H data on disk now (10/50/100/300/500/1000/1500)
   is rapidly approaching the 80% cap (~2.8 TB).** SF2000 (1.44 TB binary,
   ~530 GB parquet peak) would push us to ~4.3 TB during gen, exceeding
   /mnt/data's 3.5 TB capacity. **1.10 (gen_sf2000) will FAIL the disk-cap
   check** unless we delete some smaller scales first.

## Interpretation

SF1500 generation was the longest single experiment of the session
(90 min). With the chunked encoder it fits comfortably on this 1 TB host;
without it, this would have OOM'd python somewhere in the encode loop.
The successful gen confirms 1.1.1 is good infrastructure — every future
scale at this size or larger must use the chunked path.
