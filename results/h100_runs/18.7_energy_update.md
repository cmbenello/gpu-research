## Per-run J/GB analysis

For pure warm-run energy (excluding cold + idle):

```
Warm-only wall: 6541 + 6535 = 13.08 sec
Active GPU power during warm runs: ~280-345 W (HBM-saturated phase)
                                  ~70 W (idle phases between phases)
Estimated warm-only avg: ~200 W (rough — would need fine-grained
                                  windowing)
Energy in warm runs: 200 W × 13.08 s = 2616 J
Bytes sorted in warm: 432 GB
J/GB warm-only: 6.05
```

**Warm-only J/GB ≈ 6 J/GB** (back-of-envelope) — vastly better than
the 36-46 J/GB averaged over cold+warm runs.

The "right" number depends on the use case:
- **Cold one-shot sort**: 45.9 J/GB (this measurement)
- **Repeat-query sort** (warm): ~6 J/GB
- **Mixed cold+warm typical**: 36 J/GB (14.1 measurement)

For paper claims: **"6-46 J/GB GPU-only depending on cold/warm
ratio"**. The MendSort 2023 winner was 37 J/GB system-level. A
fair comparison would be system-level on our box, which (per 14.1)
is ~100-130 J/GB.

## Implication for paper

We're competitive on energy at warm-cache (~6 J/GB) but worse
system-level (100+ J/GB) due to GPU idle and CPU overhead.

For the headline claim: **gpu_crocsort prioritizes wall-time
throughput over absolute energy efficiency.** GPUs sort fast but
are not the most energy-efficient device per byte sorted.

The trade-off: 32.7 GB/s sustained at 6 J/GB warm vs CPU's 0.7-2.5
GB/s at maybe 5 J/GB. Net work-per-Joule is similar; throughput
strongly favors GPU.

## Caveats

- Single 3-run measurement, no repetition for variance.
- Power sampled at 200 ms intervals; sub-200ms transient peaks
  may be missed.
- Cold run dominates J/GB because of low-power CPU-heavy phases.
- A proper energy comparison needs IPMI / external watt-meter for
  system-level, not GPU-only.
