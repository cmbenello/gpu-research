## Three biggest findings (paper-quality)

1. **H100 sorts SF300 (216 GB) in 8.94 s warm at 24.2 GB/s effective** with the OVC + bitpack path. RTX 6000 OOMs at this scale. **First clean H100 win.** PCIe traffic is 50.4 GB (5× compression vs raw records).

2. **The H100 throughput curve vs scale is *non-monotonic* on this codebase** — SF50/100 underperform SF300 because the slow-path code uploads *full* 66-byte keys instead of 32-byte compact keys. After the 0.3.1 fix lands, the curve should be monotonic and SF50/100 should reach ~24 GB/s as well.

3. **The single-GPU envelope ends between SF300 (works) and SF500 (OOMs)** because both the unchunked single-pass key buffer (198 GB) and the OVC path (102.5 GB) exceed the 94 GB HBM. Multi-GPU (15.4) is the natural next architecture — 4× H100 NVL aggregate to 376 GB HBM, enough for SF500 (360 GB) entirely in HBM.
