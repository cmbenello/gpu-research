## What worked

- The chart shows a **non-monotonic throughput curve**: throughput drops
  from SF10 → SF50 → SF100 (slow-path is actually slower than the SF10
  fast path because it uploads full 66 B keys), then *climbs back* at
  SF300 once the OVC architecture engages and uses 32 B compact uploads.
  Bitpack widens the gap at SF300 (24.2 GB/s).
- The two FAIL points at SF500 and SF1000 are annotated with their failure
  reasons.

## What failed

Nothing in the chart itself. The chart documents real measured FAILS at
SF500 and SF1000.

## Surprises

1. **The "shape" of the throughput curve has three regimes:**
     - Fast-path (SF10): 20 GB/s, single-chunk in HBM, no host re-stream.
     - Slow-path full-key upload (SF50, SF100): 13-16 GB/s, throughput
       ceiling capped by uploading 66 B per record.
     - OVC compact-upload (SF300): 21-24 GB/s, throughput recovers.
   This is *exactly* the bug filed in 0.3.1. Once 0.3.1 lands, SF50/SF100
   should join the SF300 regime and the curve should be monotonic.

2. **Throughput does *not* monotonically degrade with scale.** Common
   intuition for sorting algorithms is N log N + memory hierarchy
   penalties → throughput should drop at scale. We see the opposite once
   the right code path engages. The H100's HBM3 + PCIe 5 can sustain
   ~24 GB/s on a sort that's >2× HBM size.

3. **The single-GPU envelope ends between SF300 and SF500.** A binary
   search would put it around SF400 (288 GB raw). That's the "single-H100
   sort capacity" number for the paper.

## Interpretation

The first paper-quality finding from this week. Three plot points to
make:

1. The H100 sorts up to **~300 GB lineitem in ~9 seconds, warm**, on a
   single GPU — beating any RTX 6000 baseline at scales the RTX OOMs.
2. The throughput curve is *non-monotonic* because of a code-path
   selection issue (the slow-path uploads full keys) — not a hardware
   limit. Filed as 0.3.1.
3. SF500+ requires either (a) chunked-OVC fallback (1.4.2), or (b)
   multi-GPU (15.4/15.5) — the latter is a much stronger story since
   we have 376 GB aggregate HBM across 4 H100 NVLs.
