## What worked

- All four cards are visible, idle, and pinned at persistence mode off (we'll set it before benchmarking).
- Topology shows NV6 (6-link NVLink) between *every* GPU pair — this is a full NVLink mesh, not a ring or hub-and-spoke. That changes the design space for 15.x: any GPU pair can do peer-to-peer at full NVLink bandwidth without traversing PCIe.
- NICs are split (NIC0 on NUMA 0, NIC1 on NUMA 1) but irrelevant for single-host work.

## What failed

Nothing.

## Surprises

The H100 *NVL* SKU ships with full all-to-all NVLink even on a 4-card box — I had assumed the 4-way config would be a hub topology. NV6 = 6 NVLinks per pair × ~50 GB/s/link ≈ 300 GB/s per direction per pair. That's ~10× the PCIe5 ceiling per GPU — multi-GPU sort designs that would have been bottlenecked on PCIe over a hub are not bottlenecked here. 15.2 will measure this directly.

## Interpretation

The 4-GPU sort design space is wider than I expected: with full-mesh NVLink at ~300 GB/s pair-bandwidth, all-to-all redistribution (15.6) is no longer the obvious loser vs partition-and-merge (15.4). Combined with the 1 TB host RAM and 376 GB aggregate HBM, the box can hold SF500 (~360 GB lineitem) entirely in HBM without streaming and SF1000 (~720 GB) entirely in pinned host RAM. NUMA splits the 4 GPUs into two pairs (0+1 vs 2+3) which suggests a useful ablation: same-NUMA 2-GPU vs cross-NUMA 2-GPU should differ only in host-traffic cost (15.8).
